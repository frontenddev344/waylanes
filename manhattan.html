Discover Manhattan

Manhattan puts you close to world-famous landmarks, luxury shopping, Broadway theatres, and fine dining — wherever you end up staying. Here’s what to know about the borough, and a few hotels worth a look.

Manhattan Hotels

Experience Manhattan Like Never Before

Manhattan is the borough most first-time visitors picture when they think of New York — Central Park, Fifth Avenue, Broadway, and the Midtown skyline are all within a short walk or subway ride of almost anywhere you stay here. Whether you’re visiting for business or leisure, a Manhattan base means you’re rarely more than 20 minutes from the big landmarks.

Below, we’ve picked out a couple of hotels in different parts of the borough depending on what you’re after.

Explore Manhattan

Discover New York's Most Iconic Destinations

Discover Manhattan’s most celebrated landmarks, cultural attractions, and unforgettable experiences across the borough.

01

The Peninsula New York

The Peninsula New York — a Fifth Avenue classic. Steps from Central Park and the Museum of Modern Art, this is a solid pick if you want a traditional, high-service Manhattan stay near the main shopping strip.

02

Pod 51

Pod 51 — for a smaller budget without leaving Midtown. Compact rooms, but the location near Grand Central and the East Side keeps you close to the subway and a short walk from most Midtown attractions

03

Park Lane Hotel

Park Lane Hotel — for Central Park views on a budget. Sitting right across from Central Park, Park Lane Hotel is a solid pick if you want that park-view feel without paying full luxury prices — a good base for early runs in the park or a quieter Midtown stay.

04

Dream Midtown hotel

Dream Midtown — for a design-forward stay near Broadway. On 55th Street with its distinctive Beaux-Arts exterior, Dream Midtown puts you close to Central Park, Columbus Circle, and the Theater District, with a livelier, more design-driven feel than most Midtown chains.
